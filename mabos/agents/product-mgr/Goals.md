# Goals — Product Manager

## Delegated Goals (from Stakeholder)

- **DG-PM-1**: Build a premium AI-generated wall art product line that authentically represents Kingler Bercy's artistic vision while commanding premium pricing
  - Delegated by: Kingler Bercy (Stakeholder Principal) via COO
  - Measure: 50+ piece Signature Collection launched, $79-$129 price point validated, positive customer reception
  - Timeline: 30 days for initial launch

- **DG-PM-2**: Create product experiences that differentiate VividWalls from generic AI art platforms and print-on-demand competitors
  - Delegated by: Kingler Bercy (Stakeholder Principal) via COO
  - Measure: Room visualization feature shipped, limited editions launched, measurable differentiation in customer feedback
  - Timeline: 6-month roadmap

## Strategic Goals

- **G-PM-1**: Launch Signature Collection (50+ pieces in 30 days)
  - Rationale: The Signature Collection is VividWalls' flagship product offering. It establishes brand identity, tests price points, validates style category demand, and generates initial revenue. Curated collections outperform uncurated catalogs for conversion.
  - Success criteria: 50+ pieces live on Shopify, spanning 6 style categories (60% abstract/minimalist), priced at $79-$129 for medium prints, lifestyle mockups on all product pages
  - Timeline: 30 days
  - Dependencies: Kingler's artwork availability, Creative Director approval, Inventory Manager catalog setup, CMO launch campaign

- **G-PM-2**: Implement room visualization feature (20% visitor adoption target)
  - Rationale: Room visualization / AR preview is the #1 most requested feature in wall art e-commerce. It reduces purchase hesitation by letting customers see art in their space, increases conversion, and positions VividWalls as technically innovative.
  - Success criteria: AR preview feature live on product pages, 20% of product page visitors use the feature within 30 days of launch, measurable conversion lift vs. non-users
  - Timeline: 90 days (including CTO feasibility, development, beta testing)
  - Dependencies: CTO technical evaluation, technology partner selection, CFO budget approval

## Tactical Goals

- **G-PM-3**: Establish product feedback loop with customer data
  - Rationale: Continuous product improvement requires systematic collection and analysis of customer behavior data, feature requests, satisfaction signals, and purchase patterns.
  - Success criteria: Integrated dashboard combining Shopify analytics (traffic, conversion, AOV), CS Director feedback (requests, complaints), and behavioral data (heatmaps, session recordings)
  - Timeline: 45 days after Signature Collection launch
  - Dependencies: CTO for analytics tooling, CS Director for feedback channel, sufficient traffic volume

- **G-PM-4**: Develop limited edition release cadence
  - Rationale: Limited editions (numbered prints, time-limited availability) create urgency, justify premium pricing (2-3x standard), generate press/social buzz, and build collector mentality among customers.
  - Success criteria: First limited edition released (25 numbered prints), 2x standard pricing achieved, sell-through rate >70% within release window, template process documented for repeatable releases
  - Timeline: 60 days after Signature Collection launch
  - Dependencies: Kingler's artwork selection, Creative Director for special edition presentation, CMO for launch campaign, Shopify inventory management for limited quantities

- **G-PM-5**: Optimize product page conversion rate to 3%+
  - Rationale: Product page experience directly determines revenue. Lifestyle mockups, room scene imagery, detailed size guides, substrate education, and social proof (reviews) all contribute to conversion. Industry benchmark for premium home decor is 2-4%.
  - Success criteria: Average product page conversion rate >3%, measurable through Shopify analytics, improved through iterative A/B testing
  - Timeline: 90 days (requires baseline data collection + optimization cycles)
  - Dependencies: Behavioral analytics tools, CTO for Shopify theme modifications, sufficient traffic for statistical significance

## Operational Goals

- **G-PM-6**: Maintain pricing strategy aligned with market and margin targets
  - Rationale: Pricing must balance customer perception of value, competitive positioning, and margin requirements for the $13.7M Year 5 revenue target. Regular pricing reviews prevent drift.
  - Success criteria: Quarterly pricing review incorporating competitor analysis, customer sensitivity signals, and margin data from CFO
  - Timeline: Ongoing, quarterly cadence
  - Dependencies: CFO for margin data, CMO for competitive intelligence

- **G-PM-7**: Define and maintain product taxonomy (style categories, sizes, substrates, pricing tiers)
  - Rationale: Clean product taxonomy drives effective browsing, filtering, SEO, and operational clarity across all teams.
  - Success criteria: Documented taxonomy with clear definitions, consistently applied across all Shopify products and PostgreSQL records
  - Timeline: Ongoing
  - Dependencies: Inventory Manager for catalog implementation, CTO for data consistency

- **G-PM-8**: Build seasonal collection calendar for year-round product freshness
  - Rationale: Seasonal collections (spring refresh, summer vibrancy, fall warmth, holiday gift) align with customer purchasing triggers and keep the catalog fresh.
  - Success criteria: 4 seasonal collections planned with themes, target piece counts, and launch dates aligned to demand peaks
  - Timeline: First calendar within 90 days
  - Dependencies: Kingler's artwork pipeline, Creative Director for thematic curation, CMO for campaign alignment

## Learning & Self-Improvement Goals

- **L-PM-1**: Master data-driven product management
  - Objective: Develop expert-level proficiency in using quantitative data to inform product decisions — moving from intuition-based to evidence-based product management
  - Learning path: Shopify analytics deep-dive, Google Analytics product analysis, cohort analysis techniques, conversion funnel optimization, customer segmentation methods
  - Success criteria: Can run end-to-end product analysis (hypothesis -> data collection -> analysis -> decision -> measurement) independently, with documented examples
  - Timeline: 45 days

- **L-PM-2**: Learn AR/VR integration for e-commerce
  - Objective: Understand the landscape of AR product visualization technologies, their trade-offs, and implementation requirements so that room visualization decisions are informed by technical knowledge, not just vendor claims
  - Learning path: Shopify AR capabilities and limitations, WebXR standards and browser support, Apple Vision Pro retail opportunities, third-party AR tools (Roomvo, Cylindo, Threekit), implementation cost/timeline analysis
  - Success criteria: Can evaluate AR technology options with informed technical perspective, write a requirements document for CTO, and make build-vs-buy recommendation
  - Timeline: 30 days

- **L-PM-3**: Study customer behavior analytics for visual products
  - Objective: Develop skills in interpreting how customers interact with visual product pages — what they look at, what they skip, where they drop off, and what drives purchase decisions for art
  - Learning path: Heatmap analysis (Hotjar, Microsoft Clarity), session recording interpretation, scroll depth analysis, click tracking patterns specific to gallery/portfolio layouts
  - Success criteria: Can set up behavioral tracking on product pages, identify top 3 conversion bottlenecks from data, and propose evidence-based improvements
  - Timeline: 30 days

- **L-PM-4**: Develop A/B testing expertise for product features
  - Objective: Build rigorous experimentation capability so that product changes (layouts, descriptions, pricing, features) are validated with statistical confidence rather than shipped on assumption
  - Learning path: A/B test design (hypothesis, sample size, duration), statistical significance interpretation, multi-variant testing, feature flagging implementation, avoiding common pitfalls (peeking, underpowered tests)
  - Success criteria: Can design and analyze A/B test for product page changes with proper statistical rigor, documented testing framework for team
  - Timeline: 45 days

- **L-PM-5**: Improve product positioning and pricing strategies
  - Objective: Develop sophisticated understanding of how to position and price AI-generated art products — blending art market economics with e-commerce pricing science
  - Learning path: Value-based pricing for art (perceived value vs. production cost), price anchoring techniques, tier structuring (good/better/best), limited edition pricing psychology, competitor pricing analysis methodology
  - Success criteria: Can articulate pricing strategy rationale for any product in the catalog, design pricing experiments, and recommend tier adjustments based on data
  - Timeline: 60 days
