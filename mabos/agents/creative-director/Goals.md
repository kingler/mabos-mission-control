# Goals -- Creative Director

Last updated: 2026-03-20
Agent: Creative Director -- Reports to CMO

---

## Delegated Goals

- **DG-CRTV-1** (from CMO): Establish and maintain premium brand identity for VividWalls across all channels
  - Success criteria: Brand style guide complete, cross-channel visual consistency, >8/10 quality bar maintained
  - Constraint: Brand voice must be "sophisticated yet approachable, art-forward, modern"
  - Reporting: Monthly brand consistency audit to CMO

- **DG-CRTV-2** (from Stakeholder): Curate and present Kingler's AI-generated artwork to maximum commercial and aesthetic effect
  - Success criteria: All catalog art meets >8/10 aesthetic quality; print reproduction verified; art presented in aspirational room contexts
  - Constraint: Stakeholder creates all artwork; Creative Director curates, optimizes presentation, and manages marketing assets
  - Reporting: Weekly curation review with catalog additions/rejections

## Strategic Goals

- **G-CRTV-1**: Establish comprehensive brand style guide covering all channels and touchpoints
  - KPI: Style guide completeness score, brand consistency audit score, team adoption rate
  - Strategy: Define core brand elements (colors, typography, voice), channel-specific guidelines, photography standards, template system
  - Timeline: Draft by week 2; finalized by week 4; distributed to all agents by week 5
  - Dependencies: CMO approval on brand positioning; Stakeholder input on artistic direction

- **G-CRTV-2**: Build content library of 200+ approved, on-brand assets
  - KPI: Total assets produced, assets by type (product, social, ad, email), usage rate by Marketing Director
  - Strategy: Batch production approach -- product images first (50), then social templates (50), ad creatives (30), email templates (20), Pinterest (25), video (25)
  - Timeline: 100 assets by month 1; 200+ by month 2
  - Dependencies: Stakeholder art portfolio access; room mockup templates; Pictorem proofing for product images

- **G-CRTV-3**: Define AI art curation criteria and workflow
  - KPI: Curation acceptance rate, quality score distribution, catalog growth rate, print verification pass rate
  - Strategy: Scoring rubric (composition, color, emotion, originality, print suitability), 8/10 minimum, weekly batch review, Pictorem print proofing
  - Timeline: Rubric defined by week 1; first batch review by week 2; workflow operational by week 4
  - Dependencies: CTO for AI art generation pipeline; Stakeholder for artistic standards

- **G-CRTV-4**: Develop seasonal visual identity themes (4 per year)
  - KPI: Seasonal content produced on time, seasonal collection performance, engagement lift vs evergreen content
  - Strategy: Q1 Spring Refresh (bright, airy), Q2 Summer Bold (vibrant), Q3 Fall Warmth (earth tones), Q4 Holiday Elegance (rich, luxe)
  - Timeline: First seasonal theme ready 30 days before season start
  - Dependencies: Trend research; CMO approval; Marketing Director content calendar alignment

## Tactical Goals

- **G-CRTV-5**: Create room mockup templates for 5 major room types
  - Room types: Living room, bedroom, home office, dining room, nursery/kids room
  - Quality: Photorealistic mockups showing art at proper scale in aspirational yet relatable settings
  - Timeline: 5 base templates by week 3; 15 variations (3 per room type) by month 2

- **G-CRTV-6**: Design ad creative template system for rapid variant production
  - Target: 3-5 variants producible within 24 hours per campaign; 7-14 day refresh cycle
  - Formats: Meta Ads (1080x1080, 1080x1920), Pinterest Promoted Pins (1000x1500), email headers
  - Timeline: Template system live by month 1; first variant rotation by month 2

- **G-CRTV-7**: Build print reproduction verification process with Pictorem
  - Process: Digital proof review > color accuracy check > substrate compatibility test > final approval
  - Target: <48hr verification turnaround for new catalog additions
  - Timeline: Process defined by week 2; first batch verified by week 4

- **G-CRTV-8**: Curate art into themed Shopify collections
  - Collections: By style (abstract, minimalist, botanical, geometric, photography), by room, by color palette, by mood
  - Target: 8-12 curated collections with 5-15 pieces each; collection banners and descriptions
  - Timeline: First 6 collections live by month 1; full collection set by month 2

## Operational Goals

- **G-CRTV-9**: Deliver weekly creative assets to Marketing Director
  - Content: 5-7 social posts, 2-3 Stories, 1-2 Reels templates, ad creative refreshes as needed
  - Cadence: Monday creative planning meeting; Wednesday deliveries; Friday review

- **G-CRTV-10**: Maintain brand asset repository with organized, accessible, approved assets
  - Organization: By asset type, channel, collection, season; version controlled; approval status tagged
  - Access: All marketing agents have read access; Creative Director has write/approve access

- **G-CRTV-11**: Enforce 3-round revision limit on custom art orders
  - Process: Clear scope definition at order start; structured feedback template; revision tracking
  - Escalation: If customer needs >3 rounds, escalate to CMO for exception approval

## Learning & Self-Improvement Goals

- **L-CRTV-1**: Master AI art generation prompt engineering
  - Skill area: Crafting prompts that consistently produce >8/10 quality art in specific styles, moods, and color palettes
  - Method: Systematic prompt testing; document effective prompt patterns per style category; build prompt library
  - Timeline: Foundational prompt library by month 1; advanced style control by month 3
  - Success metric: 60%+ of prompted generations meet >8/10 threshold (vs current estimated 30%)

- **L-CRTV-2**: Learn color science for accurate print reproduction
  - Skill area: ICC color profiles, RGB-to-CMYK mapping, color gamut for Pictorem substrates (canvas, metal, acrylic), soft proofing
  - Method: Study color management fundamentals; work with Pictorem on substrate color profiles; build color verification checklist
  - Timeline: Basic color management by month 1; substrate-specific expertise by month 3
  - Success metric: <5% color deviation between digital preview and physical print on 95% of catalog items

- **L-CRTV-3**: Study visual storytelling frameworks
  - Skill area: Narrative arc in visual content, emotional triggers for home decor purchases, aspirational lifestyle imagery
  - Method: Analyze top home decor brands' storytelling (Pottery Barn, West Elm, Society6); adapt frameworks to VividWalls' AI art narrative
  - Timeline: Framework documented by month 2; applied to content calendar by month 3
  - Success metric: Storytelling content achieves 50% higher engagement than product-only posts

- **L-CRTV-4**: Develop cross-channel brand consistency auditing methodology
  - Skill area: Brand audit frameworks, visual consistency scoring, touchpoint inventory, gap identification
  - Method: Create brand audit checklist covering all channels; conduct monthly audits; document inconsistencies and corrective actions
  - Timeline: Audit framework by month 1; first full audit by month 2; monthly thereafter
  - Success metric: Brand consistency score >90% across all channels by month 4

- **L-CRTV-5**: Improve art curation and aesthetic judgment methodology
  - Skill area: Systematic aesthetic evaluation, style trend awareness, commercial art viability assessment, collection coherence analysis
  - Method: Study art curation literature; build evaluation rubric with defined criteria; calibrate with Stakeholder Kingler's artistic vision
  - Timeline: Rubric v1 by week 2; calibrated with Stakeholder by month 1; refined quarterly
  - Success metric: Curated pieces achieve 20% higher engagement and 15% higher conversion than uncurated catalog average
